Daily Use Vocabulary

Polite

Showing good manners.

Always be polite to your elders.

Delicious

Having a pleasant taste.

The food smells delicious!

Hurry

To move or act with speed.

I was in a hurry to reach school.

Borrow

To take something for a short time.

Can I borrow your umbrella?

Neighbor

A person living nearby.

Our neighbor invited us to dinner.

Decide

To make a choice after thinking.

I can’t decide which movie to watch.

Medical Related Vocabulary

Prescription

A doctor's written order for medicine.

The doctor gave me a prescription.

Symptom

A sign of a disease.

Coughing is a symptom of cold.

Diagnosis

Finding out what illness someone has.

The diagnosis confirmed malaria.

Surgery

A medical operation.

He had knee surgery last week.

Injection

A shot of medicine given through a needle.

He got an injection for pain relief.

Recovery

A return to health.

She made a fast recovery after the flu.

Office Related Vocabulary

Deadline

A time limit for finishing work.

We must meet the project deadline.

Colleague

A person you work with.

My colleague helped with my report.

Promotion

Advancement to a higher position.

He got a promotion last month.

Meeting

A gathering for discussion.

We have a team meeting every Monday.

Agenda

A list of topics to discuss.

Budget review is on today's agenda.

Presentation

A talk to share information.

Her presentation was excellent.

Travel Related Vocabulary

Destination

The place you are going to.

Paris is my dream destination.

Luggage

Bags used for traveling.

Keep your luggage near you.

Passport

An official document for travel.

Don’t forget to bring your passport.

Boarding Pass

A ticket that lets you board a flight.

Show your boarding pass at the gate.

Tourist

A person traveling for pleasure.

The city was full of tourists.

Journey

The act of traveling from one place to another.

Our journey was long but enjoyable.

Education Related Vocabulary

Assignment

Work given to students.

I completed my English assignment.

Lecture

A talk given by a teacher.

Today's lecture was about grammar.

Scholarship

Money given for studying.

She received a scholarship for college.

Exam

A test of knowledge.

The math exam was difficult.

Notebook

A book for writing notes.

I wrote all my lessons in the notebook.

Teacher

A person who helps others learn.

Our teacher explained the topic clearly.

Emotional & Personality Vocabulary

Confident

Sure of oneself.

She is confident about her skills.

Honest

Truthful and sincere.

He is an honest employee.

Generous

Willing to give or share.

My friend is very generous.

Curious

Eager to know or learn something.

Children are naturally curious.

Calm

Not easily upset or excited.

He remained calm during the storm.

Grateful

Thankful and appreciative.

I’m grateful for your help.
